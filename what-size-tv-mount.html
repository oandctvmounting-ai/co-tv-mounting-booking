What Size TV Mount Do I Need? Fixed vs Tilt vs Full Motion — Complete Guide

📅 July 22, 2026 ✍️ C&O TV Mounting Team 📍 Serving Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex
Quick Answer: Match your TV's VESA pattern and weight to a mount rated for that size. For most living rooms: full-motion for corners/glare control, tilt for over-fireplace, fixed for flush, budget installs.

TV Mount Types at a Glance

Mount Type Best For Pros Cons
Fixed Flat walls, budget-friendly, clean look Lowest cost, simplest installation, minimal profile No angle adjustment, limited viewing flexibility
Tilt Over fireplace, reducing glare Vertical angle adjustment, better viewing angle than fixed No horizontal swivel, limited extension from wall
Full Motion (Articulating) Corner installations, flexible viewing angles Swivel, tilt, and extend; maximum flexibility for optimal viewing Higher cost, more complex installation, requires studs

VESA Mounting Patterns Explained

VESA (Video Electronics Standards Association) defines the standard hole pattern on the back of your TV. Common patterns include:

  • VESA 100x100 mm – for TVs up to 32"
  • VESA 200x100 mm – for mid-size TVs
  • VESA 200x200 mm – popular for 32"-55" TVs
  • VESA 400x200 mm – for larger TVs
  • VESA 400x400 mm – for 55" and larger TVs
  • VESA 600x400 mm – for extra-large TVs (65"+)

Always check your TV's manual or measure the hole pattern to ensure compatibility.

Choosing the Right Mount Size

Mount size depends on your TV's screen size and weight. Use these guidelines:

TV Size Recommended Mount Type Weight Capacity
32" - 55" Fixed, Tilt, or Full Motion Up to 75 lbs
56" - 75" Tilt or Full Motion recommended Up to 125 lbs
76" - 85" Full Motion for flexibility Up to 175 lbs
86" and above Full Motion with professional installation 200+ lbs (check specific mount)

Special Considerations

Over-Fireplace Mounting: Use a tilt mount to angle the screen downward and reduce neck strain. Ensure proper heat shielding and secure anchoring to studs.
Corner Installation: Full-motion mounts are essential for corner TVs to achieve the optimal viewing angle.
Wire Concealment: Consider internal (in-wall) or external (raceway) wire management for a clean, professional look.

Professional Installation vs DIY
